URI : the surgical strike






"URI: The Surgical Strike" is a gripping and patriotic Indian military action film released in 2019, directed by Aditya Dhar. The movie is based on the true events of the 2016 Uri attack, where four militants attacked an Indian army base in Jammu and Kashmir, resulting in significant casualties.

The film follows Major Vihaan Singh Shergill, played by Vicky Kaushal, a dedicated and skilled officer in the Indian Army who is determined to avenge the loss of his fellow soldiers. After the devastating attack, Vihaan leads a meticulously planned and executed retaliatory operation known as the surgical strike. The mission involves carrying out strategic and covert strikes on terrorist launch pads across the Line of Control (LoC) in Pakistan-administered Kashmir.

Throughout the film, "How's the josh?" becomes one of the most famous dialogues and catchphrases. It is used by Major Vihaan Singh Shergill to boost the morale of his team, and it quickly became an iconic line, expressing the soldiers' high spirits and determination during the mission.

"URI: The Surgical Strike" portrays the unwavering courage, bravery, and sacrifices of the Indian Armed Forces in defending their nation. It is a tribute to the real-life heroes who protect the integrity and security of the country. The film received critical acclaim and resonated with audiences for its powerful storytelling and tribute to the spirit of the Indian Army.
